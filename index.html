About

Journalist. Researcher. Field Reporter.

Md. Raiyan Tahrim is a journalist and researcher based in Dhaka, Bangladesh, where he works as a staff reporter for the Daily Times of Bangladesh (TOB News). His work is grounded in field reporting and public interest accountability journalism, with particular attention to stories that other outlets leave untouched.

His reporting has traversed a wide arc of beats: child protection and gender-based violence, press freedom, the Rohingya displacement crisis, urban governance failures, agricultural policy, transport economics, and the shrinking space for independent media in Bangladesh. His field dispatches from Rohingya camps in Cox's Bazar and his whistleblower protection coverage represent some of his most significant work to date.

Alongside his journalism practice, Tahrim maintains an academic research profile on ResearchGate, reflecting an interest in media studies, journalism ethics, and evidence-based reporting methodologies. He believes journalism and research are complementary, not competing, disciplines.

He approaches every story with an evidence-first framework: source verification, document review, and corroboration before publication. He does not treat official statements as settled facts, and he is committed to representing those harmed by systems of power with accuracy and care.
